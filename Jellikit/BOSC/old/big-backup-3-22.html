[MIDI Keys]
MIDI Keys is a minimalistic little nifty tool for MIDI musicians. It displays a visual keyboard on screen and lets the user interact with mouse, keyboard or external MIDI controller. Furthermore it lets you redirect the output to any MIDI port or the internal MIDI Synthesizer. You can use MIDI Keys to make your computer's keyboard act as MIDI controller (Soundtracker style), a patcher when redirecting MIDI or just to display the played notes. It also makes use of libprefs if available and supports funky roColor drops. Download.

[PedalBoard]
Pedalboard is a realtime digital audio processor that does a few effects popular with guitar players---fuzz distortion, delay (echo), and chorus. This is a rough alpha version, but still usable and a good demonstration of the power of BeOS audio streams. Works on any PowerPC BeOS machine (no Intel version for now, sorry), even a BeBox/66, at 41.1 KHz. Just plug your guitar into the audio input and jam!. Download.

[SoundPlay 2.3b5 x86]
SoundPlay is a sampleplayer that plays all files that Be's own PlaySound can play, as well as other formats such as IFF-8SVX and mpeg layer 2 and 3 files. It also works around some bugs and shortcomings of the OS, and has full pitchcontrol from -400% to 400%. As far as I know, SoundPlay is the only player in the world that can play mpeg backwards. Download.

[Liblayout 1.0]
Liblayout is a Masters Award winning library to facilitate the development of font-sensitive and resizable user interfaces. It is currently being used in for example SysInfo, SoundPlay, FontSelector, PrefServer and SetPerms (all available at your local BeWare-mirror). Liblayout is available for both PowerPC (PR2) and Pentium (R3), Available in a separate archive are documentation (still incomplete at this time), headerfiles and example programs. All three archives can be found at http://www.xs4all.nl/~marcone/be.html and will be uploaded to BeWare shortly.Marco Nelissen.
